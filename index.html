RUST MILES

Drive. Scavenge. Survive. RUST MILES is an open-world road survival game where every mile tells a story. Explore a broken America behind the wheel of whatever you can keep running.

Genre: Open-Road Survival / Exploration

// ABOUT US

Glitchhound Studio is an indie game development studio built by one person with a love for open-world games, road trips, and things that probably shouldn't work — but do. We build games that feel alive, weird, and worth exploring.

Born from the idea that the best experiences happen when you're lost somewhere you didn't plan to be — whether that's a desert highway at 3AM or a strange town that wasn't on the map.

// DEVLOG

JUN 15, 2026

Day 1: The engine starts (barely)

First day of real development. Spent most of it fighting with physics engines and wondering if procedural road generation is going to be the death of me. Got a basic vehicle controller working — it drives, kind of. The steering is more "suggestion" than "control" right now. But the car moves, the camera follows, and there's something resembling a road stretching into the distance. That's enough for day one. Tomorrow: make the car not phase through the ground.

JUN 10, 2026

Building the road system

The core of RUST MILES is the road — an endless, procedurally generated highway stretching through a broken landscape. Today I prototyped the chunk-based road system: segments load ahead and unload behind, each one potentially holding an event, a wreck, a building, or nothing at all. The emptiness is important. Long stretches of nothing make the discoveries feel real. Still working on how curves should affect terrain generation. Early results are promising but occasionally produce roads that loop into themselves, which is... a feature?

JUN 05, 2026

First car physics test

Hooked up the first real physics test for the car. It's a beat-up sedan placeholder with roughly the aerodynamic profile of a refrigerator. Suspension is way too bouncy — the car bounces off small bumps like a basketball. Managed to get it airborne off a 2-inch curb at 30mph. Need to tune damping values and tire friction. The fun part: even in its broken state, there's something satisfying about just driving. The wind audio, the engine rumble, the way the world scrolls past. That feeling is what the whole game is about. If I can nail that, the rest is details.
